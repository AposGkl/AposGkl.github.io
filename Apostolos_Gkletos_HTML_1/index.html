Lord of the Rings

Author : J.R.R Tolkien

Director: Peter Jackson

Genre: High Fantast

Year Released:2001

Awards: 17 Oscars
Fun Fact: the original idea was to make it one movie, but New Line Cinema insisted on a trilogy after seeing the budget and extent of the project

The Lord of the Rings follows the epic journey of a young hobbit named Frodo Baggins, who inherits a powerful and malevolent artifact known as the One Ring. Forged by the dark lord Sauron to control all life in Middle-earth, the Ring corrupts those who possess it. To destroy it and defeat Sauron, Frodo sets out on a perilous quest to cast it into the fires of Mount Doom, where it was made. He is aided by a fellowship of companions—including the wizard Gandalf, the ranger Aragorn, the elf Legolas, the dwarf Gimli, and his loyal hobbit friends Sam, Merry, and Pippin. As their fellowship faces betrayal, war, and immense temptation, Frodo and Sam press on alone toward Mordor. Meanwhile, their allies rally the free peoples of Middle-earth against Sauron's armies to give them a chance. In the end, the Ring is destroyed, Sauron is defeated, and peace is restored, though at great personal cost.
